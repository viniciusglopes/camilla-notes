Comparativos (Comparative Adjectives) - O MAIS IMPORTANTE!

Usamos comparativos para COMPARAR duas pessoas, animais ou coisas.

Estrutura: NOME 1 + is/are + COMPARATIVO + than + NOME 2
Exemplo: "Ana is taller than Pedro."

Regra 1 - Adjetivos curtos (1 silaba) → + ER

tall → taller short → shorter old → older long → longer smart → smarter fast → faster nice → nicer cold → colder

Regra 2 - CVC (consoante+vogal+consoante) → DOBRA + ER

big → bigger hot → hotter fat → fatter thin → thinner sad → sadder

Regra 3 - Terminados em consoante + Y → troca Y por IER

happy → happier lazy → lazier busy → busier funny → funnier heavy → heavier healthy → healthier easy → easier early → earlier sunny → sunnier naughty → naughtier

Regra 4 - Adjetivos longos (2+ silabas) → MORE + adjetivo

beautiful → more beautiful artistic → more artistic talkative → more talkative hardworking → more hardworking friendly → more friendly

Regra 5 - Irregulares (DECORAR!)

good → better (melhor) bad → worse (pior) far → further (mais longe)
💡 DICA DE OURO: Conte as silabas!
• 1 silaba = + ER (tall, short, fast)
• 2 silabas terminando em Y = troca Y por IER (happy, funny)
• 2+ silabas = MORE + adjetivo (beautiful, artistic)

Parte C - Coloque as palavras em ordem:
1. than / winter / hotter / summer / is → Summer is hotter than winter.

3. is / older / than / Sydney / Rome → Rome is older than Sydney.

5. are / stronger / than / wolves / dogs → Wolves are stronger than dogs.
6. is / English / easier / than / Japanese → English is easier than Japanese.

Interpretacao de Texto

📖 DICAS:
• Ler o texto inteiro primeiro
• Sublinhar as palavras-chave
• Voltar ao texto para conferir cada afirmativa
• Prestar atencao em: always, never, sometimes, every day
Exercicio - Leia o texto e classifique TRUE ou FALSE:

"I'm Paula and I like playing the guitar and singing. My sister Amy loves chatting with her friends on the phone. She likes playing football at the weekends. My brother Dan is good at Maths and Science. My brother and I enjoy doing homework, but our sister doesn't like school. We've got a big dog called Sandy. She's five years old and she likes playing with us. We've got a small black cat too. His name's Alfie and he's ten years old. He doesn't enjoy playing with us."

1. Paula likes playing football. → FALSE (Amy likes football)
2. Amy is talkative. → TRUE (she loves chatting on the phone)
3. Dan is clever. → TRUE (good at Maths and Science)
4. Sandy is a small cat. → FALSE (Sandy is a big dog)
5. Alfie is older than Sandy. → TRUE (Alfie = 10 years, Sandy = 5 years)
6. Paula is more artistic than Amy. → TRUE (Paula plays guitar and sings)

Resumo Final - Checklist para a Prova

  • Adjetivos: saber o significado e os opostos
  • Comparativos curtos: + ER (tall → taller)
  • Comparativos CVC: dobra + ER (big → bigger)
  • Comparativos com Y: troca Y por IER (happy → happier)
  • Comparativos longos: MORE + adjetivo (more beautiful)
  • Irregulares: good → better, bad → worse, far → further
  • Sempre usar THAN para comparar!
  • Interpretacao: ler com atencao, voltar ao texto
  • Completar frases: usar o contexto da frase
